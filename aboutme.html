About Me

Education & Career Goals

As a recent Computer Science graduate, I have a strong foundation in software engineering principles and experience working on real-world projects. At Kaluza, I began developing experience using agile methodologies and gained proficiency in building large-scale web applications using React and Typescript. I then went on to apply my skills and experience in a more hands-on role and taking lead for DrawDojo, a large-scale university group project built using React and Typescript. During this same year, my third year at Heriot Watt, I was awarded the Heriot Watt Deputy Principle Award for achieving a minimum of 6 A grades out of the 8 modules I sat.

I hope to work in an environment that fosters continuous learning and growth, and allows me to work on interesting and impactful projects. I am open to different sectors within software engineering, whether it be web development, mobile development, or software engineering in general. My ultimate goal is to become a well-rounded software engineer who can tackle complex problems with ease and contribute to meaningful projects that make a positive impact on society.

With proficiency in various object-oriented programming languages such as Java, C#, and C++, I am excited to contribute to a professional environment and continue to build upon my skill set.

Hobbies & Interests

In my free time, I love problem solving and often find myself looking for puzzles or other problems to solve. I enjoy challenging myself and learning new things, whether it's a new programming language or a difficult riddle. I am also interested in subjects related to the VFX industry, not so much the design aspect, but the technical aspect such as light or fluid simulations, and enjoy learning about new advancements and techniques in the field.

Outside of the technical world, I also enjoy spending time in nature. I love taking walks through the countryside or by the sea, and I find it a great way to unwind after a long day. I have a passion for travel and exploring new places, and I am always looking for opportunities to see more of the world. I'm also a certified PADI scuba diver, although being a student has made it difficult to pursue this hobby as often as I'd like. Overall, I believe that pursuing hobbies and interests outside of work is important for maintaining a healthy work-life balance and for keeping my mind sharp and creative.
